Montenegro has two very different personalities depending on the season: a packed, hot Adriatic coast in summer, and a quiet, dramatic mountain interior nearly year-round.

## June and September: the sweet spot

Warm enough to swim, cool enough to hike, and noticeably fewer crowds and lower prices than July or August. If you only take one piece of advice from this post, it's this.

## July and August: peak season

Hot, busy, and the most expensive time to book hotels and cars. Kotor's Old Town gets very crowded when cruise ships dock. Worth it if your only free time is summer — just book accommodation early.

## Winter (November–March)

The coast is quiet and mild by northern European standards, while Durmitor National Park in the north gets proper snow — good for a different kind of trip entirely.

## Bottom line

For most first-time visitors: aim for the last two weeks of June or the first three weeks of September.
