The honest answer: it depends on how much of the interior you want to see, and how much you mind being tied to bus timetables.

## If you're staying on the coast

Buses run frequently between Herceg Novi, Kotor, Budva, and Bar, and are cheap and easy to use without local language. If your trip is coastline-only — Old Towns, beaches, boat trips — you can skip the rental entirely and save the money and hassle of mountain driving.

## If you want to see the interior

Durmitor, Lovćen, the Tara Canyon, and most of the country's best viewpoints are poorly served by public transport, if at all. A car (or a driver for a day) is close to essential if these are on your list.

## What driving is actually like

Coastal roads are narrow, twisty, and busy with tour buses in summer — slower going than the map distances suggest. Mountain roads are well-paved but have serious drop-offs and hairpin turns; take them slowly and avoid driving them after dark on a first visit.

## Bottom line

Coast-only trip: skip the car. Anything involving the mountains: rent one, or book a driver for the specific day trips that need it.
