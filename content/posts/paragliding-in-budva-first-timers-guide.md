The first time you run off a hillside above Bečići and feel the ground drop away, your brain still expects to fall. It doesn't. Tandem paragliding in Budva is one of the few genuinely thrilling things you can do here with zero experience, zero training, and about an hour of your afternoon — and it's become one of the most-booked adventure activities on this stretch of the Adriatic.

## Where you actually fly from

Almost every operator launches from Brajići, a hillside above Bečići at roughly 760–790 meters, about 15 km and a 20-minute drive from the beach. You'll meet at the landing area on Bečići beach first, then get driven up in a van — the ride alone gives you a preview of the coastline you're about to see from the air. You launch by jogging a few steps downhill with your pilot strapped in behind you, and you land back on the sand at Bečići, often within sight of your beach towel.

The flight path traces the Budva Riviera: Bečići's long beach below you, Budva's Old Town and citadel to one side, and on a clear day, the silhouette of Sveti Stefan further down the coast.

## What it actually costs and how long it takes

Budget somewhere in the €50–150 range depending on the operator and package — flight-only options sit at the lower end, while packages with GoPro photos/video, hotel pickup, or an extended flight time push toward the higher end. The whole outing takes about an hour door to door: a short briefing, the drive up, the flight itself, and the walk back to your towel. The actual time in the air is typically 15–25 minutes, longer if thermal conditions are good that day.

Most operators take payment after the flight rather than a non-refundable deposit upfront, and many will reschedule for free if the wind isn't cooperating — which happens more than you'd think on a coastline this exposed.

## Who can actually do this

There's no licence, training, or fitness test required — the pilot does the flying, you just run a few steps at takeoff and sit back for the rest. Age and weight limits vary by operator, but as a rough guide: most fly kids from around 5–6 years old with a parent's consent, adults well into their 60s and 70s, and passengers up to somewhere around 100–120 kg (check with your chosen operator if you're near that upper limit, since wind conditions on the day can shift what they're comfortable with). Pregnant passengers generally aren't flown, for obvious reasons.

If you're nervous about heights, know that the launch and landing are the only physically active parts — once you're airborne, you're just sitting in a harness taking in the view.

## Picking the right time of day

Morning and early evening flights tend to be smoother, with calmer air and less bounce — good if you want a gentle first flight or you're prone to motion sickness. Midday and early afternoon bring stronger thermals, which pilots use to gain more altitude and give you a longer, more adventurous ride if that's what you're after.

Season runs roughly April through October, with July and August obviously the busiest and warmest. If you're visiting during a festival week — Sea Dance in Budva or the new EXIT Montenegro dates both land in July this year — book your flight for a weekday morning if you can, since operators get booked out fast and beach parking near the meeting point fills up early.

## What to bring

Closed shoes or sneakers are non-negotiable for the run at takeoff — flip-flops aren't safe here. It's noticeably windier and cooler in the air than on the beach, so bring a light jacket or windbreaker even in summer, plus sunglasses. Most pilots fly with a GoPro and will sell you the footage afterward, so you don't strictly need to bring your own camera, though some operators let you mount your own if it's secured with a strap.

## Bottom line

Paragliding in Budva is genuinely one of the easiest adventure activities to just show up and do — no training, a short time commitment, and a launch site close enough to the beach that it fits into an ordinary day trip. Book a morning slot for calmer air if it's your first flight, wear proper shoes, and check with the operator directly if you're near the upper weight range. If you're staying anywhere along the Budva Riviera, a rental car makes it easy to combine the flight with a stop in Old Town or a swim at Bečići afterward, rather than relying on local buses to get you there and back.
