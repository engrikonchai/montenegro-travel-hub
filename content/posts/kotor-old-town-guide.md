Kotor's Old Town sits inside Venetian-era walls, tucked at the back of a bay that's often (technically incorrectly) called Europe's southernmost fjord.

## Getting there

Most visitors base themselves in Kotor town itself, or in Budva and take a day trip. Both work — Kotor is compact enough to see properly in a day.

## The walls walk

The climb up the city walls to the Fortress of San Giovanni is the single most-recommended thing to do here, and it earns the hype. Go at sunrise or late afternoon to avoid both the heat and the cruise-ship crowds.

## Where it gets crowded

When two or three cruise ships dock on the same morning, the old town's narrow streets fill up fast. Check the day's cruise schedule if you want a quieter visit.
