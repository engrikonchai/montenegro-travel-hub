Durmitor is the part of Montenegro most coastal visitors never see, and it's worth the detour if you have more than a beach-and-Old-Towns trip in mind.

## Where to base yourself

Žabljak, the highest town in the Balkans, is the obvious base — small, close to the trailheads, with enough guesthouses and restaurants to spend a few nights comfortably.

## The must-do hikes

Black Lake (Crno Jezero) is the easy, everyone-does-it walk — flat, scenic, and doable in under two hours. For something more serious, the climb up Bobotov Kuk, Durmitor's highest peak, is a full-day hike with real elevation gain and genuine mountain views at the top.

## When to go

Snow can linger into June at altitude, and the first snow can arrive by October — the reliable hiking window is roughly late June through mid-September. Outside that, check trail conditions before committing to anything above Black Lake.

## Getting there

It's a mountain drive from the coast, roughly 2.5–3 hours from Kotor, on roads that are well-paved but slow going with the hairpin turns. Budget the whole day for the drive alone if you're doing it as part of a longer coastal trip.

## Bottom line

Worth 2–3 nights if your trip has room for it — pair Black Lake with a coastal-only day and save Bobotov Kuk for visitors who actually want a serious hike.
