Both towns show up on nearly every Montenegro itinerary, and both make reasonable bases — but they suit different kinds of trips.

## Kotor: history and day trips

Kotor sits at the back of the bay, inside intact city walls, with the fortress walk as its signature activity. It's the better base if you want walkable history, easy day trips to Perast and Our Lady of the Rocks, and a quieter evening scene once the cruise ships leave.

## Budva: beaches and nightlife

Budva has its own (smaller) old town, but its real draw is the beach and bar scene — livelier at night, with more resort-style accommodation nearby. It's the better base if beach time and nightlife matter more to you than historic sightseeing.

## Getting between them

They're about 40 minutes apart by car or bus, close enough that basing in one doesn't rule out a day trip to the other.

## Bottom line

History-and-quiet-evenings trip: base in Kotor. Beach-and-nightlife trip: base in Budva. Either way, budget a half-day for the other.
